Your diet score is the percentage of rules you followed across all days in the week — so if you have 7 rules and checked all of them every day (7 days), that's 100%. Missing one rule on one day brings the score down proportionally. The score adapts automatically as you add or remove rules.

Running programme
Endurance · Fat burn · Cardio base
Runs are the primary calorie-burning activity. Three types: easy runs build aerobic base, tempo runs build lactate threshold, and incline walks are low-impact active recovery. Log distance, time, and how you felt on each run.
Easy run
PhaseTargetPace feelNotes
Phase 1 (Wks 1–4)
20–25 min continuous
Conversational
You should be able to hold a conversation. If you can't, slow down. Heart rate around 65–75% max. Focus on consistency, not speed.
Phase 1 (Wks 5–8)
25–35 min · building to 5km
Conversational
Add 5 min per week. By week 8 you should be running 5km without stopping. Don't increase pace — increase duration only.
Phase 1 (Wks 9–10)
5km · ~30–35 min
Comfortable
Consolidate the 5km. Don't push pace yet. The goal is to make 5km feel easy before Phase 2 adds tempo work.
Phase 2
5–6km · 2× per week
Comfortable
Mon and Fri easy runs. Keep these genuinely easy — they complement the harder tempo session mid-week. Don't turn easy days into moderate days.

Burns ~300 kcal with zero impact on joints or recovery. Good substitute on days when legs are heavy from running. Don't hold the rails — let your legs and glutes do the work.

45 min
10–12% incline · 5–6 km/h

10% rule: Never increase weekly running volume (total km or total time) by more than 10% from one week to the next. This is the most effective way to avoid injury. Back pain during a run: Stop immediately, walk home, rest the next day.
Session A — Push
Chest · Shoulders · Triceps Tue · ~45 min
Chest, shoulders, and triceps. All pushing movements. No spinal loading — bench press performed lying flat, shoulder press seated. Leg press at the end keeps lower body active without stressing the back.
Warm-up (5 min): 2 min light cross trainer · 10 arm circles each direction · 10 band pull-aparts or light lateral raises · 10 bodyweight squats
ExerciseSets × repsNotes
Dumbbell flat bench press
Dumbbells · mat or bench
3 × 10–12
Feet flat. Lower until elbows reach 90° — don't flare wide. Press straight up. Don't arch the lower back off the bench.
Back-safe
Pec fly machine
Pec fly machine
3 × 12–15
Seat height: handles at mid-chest. Slight bend in elbows — never lock out. Squeeze at peak, return slowly (3 sec). Zero spinal load.
Back-safe
Seated dumbbell shoulder press
Dumbbells · seat with back support
3 × 10–12
Back supported. Start at ear height, press up without locking out. Don't lean back to push — if you are, weight is too heavy.
Back-safe
Dumbbell lateral raise
Light dumbbells
3 × 12–15
Slight bend in elbows. Raise to shoulder height only. Lead with elbows, not wrists. Shoulders down throughout. Go lighter than you think.
Back-safe
Tricep cable pushdown
Cable pulldown machine (rope or bar)
3 × 12–15
Cable at highest position. Elbows tucked to sides — they don't move. Push to full extension, squeeze, return slowly. Torso upright.
Back-safe
Overhead dumbbell tricep extension
Single dumbbell (seated)
3 × 12
Hold one dumbbell with both hands overhead. Lower behind head slowly, elbows forward. Extend back up. Good stretch at the bottom.
Back-safe
Leg press
Leg press machine (or 3×15 bodyweight squats)
3 × 15
Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Lower to 90° — no deeper. Don't lock out knees at top. Lower back pressed into seat throughout.
Back-safe
Rest: 60–75 sec for isolation · 90 sec for compound moves. Progressive overload: Complete all reps cleanly for 2 sessions in a row → add 1–2kg next session.
Session B — Pull
Back · Biceps · Rear delts Fri · ~45 min
Back, biceps, and rear delts. The cable pulldown is your primary back tool — it replaces pull-ups and is more controllable for building lat strength. Face pulls are non-negotiable for desk posture and shoulder health.
Warm-up (5 min): 2 min light cross trainer · 10 cat-cows · dead hang from pull-up bar 10–15 sec (spinal decompression) · 10 arm circles · shoulder rolls
ExerciseSets × repsNotes
Cable lat pulldown (wide grip)
Cable pulldown machine
4 × 10–12
Grip just outside shoulder width. Lean back 10–15° only. Pull bar to upper chest — never behind neck. Elbows drive down and back. Squeeze lats at bottom, return slowly. Your primary back builder.
Back-safe
Cable lat pulldown (close grip)
Cable pulldown · neutral grip attachment
3 × 12
Narrow neutral grip. Elbows drive to hips. Hits lower lat and teres major. Slightly heavier than wide grip for most people.
Back-safe
Single-arm dumbbell row
Dumbbell · bench for support
3 × 10–12 each
Knee and hand on bench, back flat. Row dumbbell to hip — not shoulder. Think "elbow to ceiling". Don't rotate the torso to lift heavier. Squeeze 1 sec at top.
Back-safe
Cable face pull
Cable pulldown · rope attachment at face height
3 × 15
Pull rope to forehead, elbows flaring out and back. External rotation at the top. Light weight, full control. Directly counteracts desk posture. Do not skip.
Back-safe
Dumbbell rear delt fly
Light dumbbells
3 × 15
Hinge forward ~45°, knees soft. Raise both arms to shoulder height. Return slowly. Very light weight. If lower back feels it, do seated bent-over instead.
Keep torso braced
Dumbbell bicep curl (alternating)
Dumbbells
3 × 12 each
Elbows pinned to sides. Supinate (rotate palm up) as you curl. Lower 2 sec. If you're swinging the torso, weight is too heavy.
Back-safe
Hammer curl
Dumbbells
2 × 12
Palms face each other throughout. Works brachialis and brachioradialis. Can go slightly heavier than regular curl. Alternate arms.
Back-safe
Step-ups with dumbbells
Dumbbells · sturdy bench or step
3 × 10 each leg
Drive through heel of front foot. Don't push off back foot. Step down with control. Loads glutes and quads without spinal compression. Alternate leading leg each set.
Back-safe
Rest: 60 sec for isolation · 90 sec for compound. Pull-up bar: Dead hang 10–15 sec as warm-up. From week 8: jump to top, lower slowly over 5 sec (assisted negative) as a bonus set.

Romanian deadlift (dumbbell)
Dumbbells — moderate weight
3 × 12
Hinge at hips, lower along shins to mid-shin. Back flat throughout. Start light (8–10kg each). Only increase weight when form is perfect. Stop immediately if back complains.
Phase 2 only — form before weight
Rest: 90–120 sec after goblet squat and RDL · 60–90 sec for upper body compound · 45–60 sec for isolation. Deload every 4th week: drop weights 20%, reduce sets by 1.

Pull-led full body. Higher volume on back and biceps. Lunges as primary leg movement — loads glutes and quads without spinal compression. Rear delt and face pull keeps shoulder health on track.

Session C — Full body (leg emphasis)
Legs · Glutes · Shoulders · Arms Sat · ~55 min
Dedicated leg emphasis session — the answer to Phase 1 having no dedicated leg day. Step-ups, goblet squats, and glute bridges form a complete lower body routine with no barbell or spinal compression. If Sat is a padel day, swap Session C to Fri and take Sat as rest.
Warm-up (5–7 min): 3 min treadmill incline walk (10%, 5.5 km/h) · 10 cat-cows · 15 bodyweight glute bridges · 10 bodyweight goblet squats · 10 leg swings each side

4 × 10
Heavier than Session A. 2-sec pause at the bottom (pause squat) to increase difficulty without adding more weight. This is the primary quad and glute builder today.

Step-ups (heavy)
Heavy dumbbells · bench
4 × 10 each leg
Heaviest step-ups in the programme. 1-sec pause at the top (standing on bench). Drive through heel. No pushing off back foot at all. Alternate leading legs between sets.

Romanian deadlift (heavier)
Dumbbells

Dumbbell lateral lunge
Single dumbbell (goblet hold)
3 × 10 each side
Step wide to one side, sit into that hip — other leg stays straight. Push back to centre. Hits inner thighs and glute medius — often weak from desk sitting.
Back-safe
Single-leg glute bridge
Mat · bodyweight or dumbbell on hip
3 × 12 each
One leg extended, drive hips up with planted foot. Hold 2 sec at top. If hips drop to one side, keep working on it — that's a weakness to fix. Add dumbbell once bodyweight is easy.

Dumbbells
3 × 10
Secondary shoulder volume. Superset with lateral raises to save time: press 10 reps → lateral raise 12 reps immediately → rest 75 sec.
Back-safe
Bicep curl + tricep pushdown superset
Dumbbells + cable

Alternate curl and pushdown with no rest between — rest 60 sec after both. Arms get volume without a dedicated session. Efficient finisher.

Daily core routine
Every day · 10–15 min
Do every morning before eating if possible. These exercises build deep spinal stabilisers without loading or flexing the spine — the foundation of back rehab. Order matters: cat-cow first, then progress through the list.
Order matters: Cat-cow (mobilise) → dead bug → bird dog → glute bridge → plank → side plank. Never skip cat-cow — 90 seconds of spinal mobilisation before anything else.
ExerciseSets × repsNotes
Cat-cow
Mat · bodyweight
2 × 10 breaths
Inhale: drop belly, lift head and tailbone (cow). Exhale: round spine up, tuck chin (cat). 3 sec each direction. Spinal mobilisation — do even on rest days.
Do every single morning
Dead bug
Mat · bodyweight
3 × 8 each side
Arms up, knees at 90°. Lower opposite arm and leg — lower back stays pressed into mat throughout. If it arches, you've gone too far. Teaches core to stabilise while limbs move — exactly what running demands.
Primary back rehab
Bird dog
Mat · bodyweight
3 × 8 each side
On hands and knees, back flat. Extend opposite arm and leg, hold 3 sec. Imagine balancing a glass of water on your lower back — don't let it tip. Builds the multifidus — deepest spinal stabiliser.
Primary back rehab
Glute bridge
Mat (add dumbbell on hips from wk 4)
3 × 15
Drive hips up, squeeze glutes hard, hold 2 sec, lower slowly. Progress to single-leg from wk 5. Weak glutes = primary cause of lower back pain. This directly fixes it.
Back rehab — glute strength
Forearm plank
Mat · bodyweight
3 × 20–60 sec
Squeeze everything — glutes, abs, quads. Breathe normally. Hips don't sag or rise. Add 5 sec each week. Once at 60 sec, switch to harder variations rather than going longer.
Back-safe
Side plank
Mat · bodyweight
3 × 20–45 sec each
Add from week 3. Body in a straight line — hips don't sag. Works quadratus lumborum, the muscle along the side of your lumbar spine. Weak QL is a common cause of one-sided back pain.
Add from week 3
Permanently avoid: Sit-ups · Crunches · Russian twists · Straight-leg raises · Any exercise causing or worsening back pain
